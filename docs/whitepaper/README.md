# Smart Classroom White Paper

This folder collects the raw material for the class's end-of-semester white paper and the companion flow website. Everything here is written **by you** — your projects, your failures, your screenshots, your words.

The white paper tells the honest story of what the class built this semester: what we tried, what worked, what broke, and what a real product would look like. It only works if every student contributes their piece.

## Folder layout

```
docs/whitepaper/
├── README.md              <- you are here
├── retros/                <- one retrospective per student
├── glossary.md            <- shared table, one term per student
├── vignettes/             <- "a moment in the room" paragraphs
├── journey-map-annotated.png  <- shared FigJam export
├── artifacts/             <- screenshots, clips, photos, log excerpts
├── interviews/            <- peer interview writeups
└── diagrams/              <- hand-drawn diagram photos
```

## How to contribute

1. **Fork or branch** the repo, don't commit directly to `main`.
2. Put your files in the folder named above.
3. Follow the **naming convention**: `<your-firstname>-<project-slug>-<description>.<ext>`
   - ✅ `phil-timer-screenshot.png`
   - ✅ `juju-gus-retro.md`
   - ❌ `Screenshot 2026-04-10 at 3.47.12 PM.png`
4. Open a pull request when your assignment is done.
5. If you get stuck, ask in the class Discord — don't let a git issue block your submission.

## Assignments overview

One bundled assignment per week. Each week has a theme and a small set of deliverables that fit together.

| Week | Theme | Deliverables | Time range | Due |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | **Tell your story** | Project retrospective (`retros/`) + one glossary entry (`glossary.md`) | **1–3 hrs** | **Sun Apr 19** |
| **Week 2** | **Capture the reality** | Artifact capture — screenshot, short clip, setup photo, Discord log excerpt (`artifacts/`) + annotate the shared journey map (`journey-map-annotated.png`) | **1–3 hrs** | **Sun Apr 26** |
| **Week 3** | **Explain it to someone else** | "A moment in the room" vignette (`vignettes/`) + peer interview in pairs (`interviews/`) + hand-drawn system diagram (`diagrams/`) | **1.5–4.5 hrs** | **Sun May 3** |
| **Week 4** | **Synthesis & showcase** | The white paper is assembled, everyone reviews their own sections, we prep the live showcase | — | Starts **Mon May 4** |

> **About the time ranges:** the low end is "you know exactly what you want to say and knock it out." The high end (roughly triple) is "you want to think hard, iterate, and hand in something you're proud of." Both are valid — pick your pace.

> **Coordinate across groups (especially Week 2).** Some of the best artifacts need more than one person in the room at the same time. If you want a video of someone's **timer** project reacting to a real student, or a clip of the room shifting into **lecture mode** when people sit down, you'll need to schedule that with the people involved. Post in the class Discord early in the week: *"I'm capturing my artifact Thursday at 3pm in room X — come be a body in the frame?"* Trade favors. The stories that make the white paper interesting are the ones where projects touch each other, and those don't happen if everyone captures alone.

## Ground rules

- **Honesty beats polish.** "My detector thought my coffee mug was a person" is more valuable than "it worked great."
- **Your voice.** Don't write like a textbook. Write like you're explaining your project to a friend who doesn't code.
- **Credit your teammates.** If someone helped you debug or gave you an idea, name them.
- **No AI-generated content** for the retros, vignettes, or interviews. You can use AI tools to check grammar, but the words should be yours — the point of this exercise is to capture *your* thinking.

## What happens to this material

Everything in this folder becomes part of:

1. **The white paper** — a ~12-page illustrated document telling the semester's story, shared with faculty and future students.
2. **The flow website** — a plain-language explainer at `docs/flow/` showing how all the pieces talk to each other.
3. **The public showcase** — published alongside the existing slide decks at the class documentation site.
