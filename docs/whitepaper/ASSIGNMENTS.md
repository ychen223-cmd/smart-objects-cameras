## Part 1 — Project Retrospective (45 min – 2.25 hrs)

Write a one-page honest retro of the project you built this semester.

**Steps:**
1. Copy `docs/whitepaper/retros/TEMPLATE.md` to a new file named `<yourfirstname>-<project>-retro.md` in the same folder.
   - Example: `phil-timer-retro.md`
2. Fill in every section. Keep it short — two or three sentences per block is enough.
3. Grab one screenshot of your project running, save it to `docs/whitepaper/artifacts/` using the name `<yourfirstname>-<project>-screenshot.png`, and link it from your retro.
4. Open a PR titled `retro: <yourfirstname> <project>`.

**The section that matters most is "What broke."** Be specific and honest — false positives, weird edge cases, things a future student should know. "My detector thought my coffee mug was a person" is more valuable than "it worked great." This is the single most important section of the whole white paper.

**Do NOT use AI to write your retro.** Grammar check is fine, but the words should be yours. The point is to capture *your* thinking.

## Part 2 — Glossary Entry (20 min – 1 hr)

Pick one technical term from `docs/whitepaper/glossary.md` and explain it so a non-engineer can understand it.

**Steps:**
1. Open `docs/whitepaper/glossary.md`.
2. Pick a term from the table that doesn't have an Owner yet. **First come, first served** — claim it by putting your name in the Owner column in your PR.
3. Write two sentences of plain-language definition. No jargon inside the definition — if you need another technical term, pick a different word.
4. Add one analogy from everyday life (cooking, sports, mail, weather — anything). See the "EAR" example at the bottom of the file for the quality bar.
5. Open a PR titled `glossary: <term>`.

If you're not sure what your term means, that's fine — go learn it first, then write the definition. Don't guess.

## Why we're doing this

The white paper and flow website are for faculty, future students, and people who've never touched DepthAI. Your retros become the "what we tried / what worked / what broke" section of the paper. Your glossary entries become the appendix and the website tooltips. If we leave these blank, the whole document falls over.

Your name will be on your contributions — this is a portfolio piece.
