# Plain-Language Glossary

One of you owns each row. Your job: explain your assigned term in **two sentences a non-engineer can understand**, then add **one analogy** that makes it stick.

**Rules:**
- No jargon inside your definition. If you need another technical term, define that one too or pick a different word.
- The analogy should be something from everyday life — cooking, sports, mail, weather, anything.
- If you genuinely don't know what your term means yet, that's fine — go learn it first, then write the definition. Don't guess.

**How to claim a term:** put your name in the "Owner" column via a pull request. First come, first served. If you want a term that's already taken, pick another one.

## Example of what good looks like

| Term | Owner | Plain-language definition (2 sentences) | Analogy |
|---|---|---|---|
| EAR (Eye Aspect Ratio) | Kevin | A number between 0 and 1 that says how open a person's eye is. If it stays low for about two seconds, the camera guesses the person is tired. | Think of an eye as a curtain. Fully open is around 0.3, almost closed is near 0 — if the curtain stays mostly drawn, it's probably nap time. |

That's the bar. Two honest sentences plus one memorable analogy. Aim for something a middle-schooler would understand.

---

## Why we're doing this

The white paper and the flow website are for faculty, future students, and people who've never touched DepthAI. If we leave the technical terms undefined, those readers bounce off on the first page. Your definition will show up in the final paper as a glossary and on the website as hover tooltips — so take it seriously, this is the part non-technical readers will actually use.
